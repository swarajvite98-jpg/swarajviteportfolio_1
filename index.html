Projects

Smart Water Distribution and Networking in Apartment

This project focuses on one of the important urban sustainability challenges: efficient and fair distribution of water in apartment societies and residential complexes.

The proposed system is designed to ensure more equitable water distribution across all apartments using smart sensors, real-time monitoring, and automated control methods. The system aims to detect leakages, monitor water usage, and regulate pressure to reduce wastage while maintaining better distribution efficiency.

The project also introduces the concept of Internet of Things-based monitoring in water distribution networks, which can improve control, support dynamic response to changing water demand, and promote sustainable water management in society-level infrastructure.

This project reflects my interest in combining engineering concepts with smart monitoring systems to improve resource management and sustainability.

CFD Simulation of Double Pipe Heat Exchanger

This project involved the simulation and analysis of heat transfer mechanisms in a double pipe heat exchanger using ANSYS Fluent 16.2.

Heat exchangers are widely used in industries to transfer thermal energy efficiently, and simulation helps in understanding how flow behavior and heat transfer interact inside such systems. In this project, I studied how computational fluid dynamics can be used to model the exchanger and analyze thermal performance.

The project strengthened my understanding of heat transfer concepts, fluid flow behavior, thermal energy recovery, and the practical importance of simulation tools in engineering design.

It also improved my knowledge of ANSYS Fluent and showed how software-based analysis can support industrial process optimization.
